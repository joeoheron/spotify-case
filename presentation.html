Five major labels managed a tightly integrated value chain, where they controlled everything between talent management, recording, manufacturing, distribution, and retail.

Disruption

Napster & the Loss of Control

1999
Napster comes onto the scene with 80M MP3 files and gains 26.4M users
2001
The Recording Industry Association of America shuts down Napster, but KaZaA, LimeWire, and BitTorrent emerge and evade without central servers
2008
The industry drops to $15.9B — down 37% from peak
2008
Spotify launches as a Swedish startup, with Europe as its first market
PFI Lens

The CD was the entire value capture mechanism. Once digital, labels' complementary assets evaporated.

Free-Fall

Industry in Free-Fall

Napster's closure decentralised piracy — KaZaA, LimeWire, BitTorrent emerged without central servers.

The Misdiagnosis

30,000+ consumer lawsuits by the RIAA: a PR catastrophe. The industry treated it as a legal problem — it was a business model mismatch.

The Real Problem

Users didn't refuse to pay — they refused to pay $18 for one song. Incumbents with sunk costs in physical infrastructure couldn't cannibalise their own model.

PFI Lens

Strong appropriability requires either IP protection or bottleneck assets. Napster destroyed both. A textbook 'pre-paradigmatic' phase of high uncertainty.

Appropriability

70%

Teece's Term

Dominant Design

Three Phases of Evolution

Before dominant design, competition centres on technology. After, it shifts to process and complementary assets.

Phase 1 · Fluid

Pre-Design

Multiple competing architectures. High uncertainty. First-mover advantage is real but temporary.

2001 – 2011
Phase 2 · Transition

Emergence

On-demand streaming displaces downloads. Network effects and a breakout product narrow competition.

2011 – 2015
Phase 3 · Specific

Post-Design

Architecture established. Advantage derives from execution and assets — not being first.

2015 → Present

Critical: Apple Music replicated Spotify's design within months and reached 10M subscribers in six weeks via iOS pre-installation. Teece's prediction in action.

Asset Types

Complementary Assets: Teece's Typology

GENERICNo competitive advantage

Freely available at market rates. Cloud infrastructure, broadband, software talent. Any entrant acquires them at the same cost.

SPECIALISEDUnilaterally dependent

Valuable in context of this technology. Streaming licences, algorithmic discovery, podcast studios. Eventually matchable by well-resourced rivals.

CO-SPECIALISEDBilaterally dependent — the strongest moats

Asset and innovation mutually enhance each other. Apple's App Store + iOS ecosystem. Amazon Prime bundle. The primary value capture mechanism post-dominant-design.

R1 · Appropriability ↑

Own the Content Pipeline

In a weak appropriability regime, value capture requires proprietary assets competitors cannot licence.

Podcasts: Joe Rogan demonstrated viability — drove premium growth in 18–35 demo. Continue exclusive strategy.

Audiobooks: Expand via Findaway (acquired 2021). Growing market dominated by Audible — no streaming incumbent.

Originals: Commission audio dramas and artist-exclusive sessions — co-specialised content only on Spotify.

Unit economics: Owned podcast plays generate returns through retention without triggering any per-play royalty.

R2 · Assets ↑

Monetise Proprietary Data

30B+
Listening events per day
356M
Users generating data

Tiered Data Products

Expand Spotify for Artists: free for independents, premium for labels, managers, booking agents.

B2B Intelligence

Listening context (mood, activity, time-of-day) is qualitatively superior to demographic targeting.

Governance: All data monetisation must comply with GDPR, CCPA, and emerging privacy regulation. Opt-in is non-negotiable.

R3 · Appropriability ↑ · Royalty Leverage

Direct Artist Relationships

When a bottleneck is held by a concentrated oligopoly, the innovator should build alternative supply routes.

Distribution: Expand Spotify for Artists into full direct distribution — competing with DistroKid and TuneCore.

Promo tools: Formalise Marquee and algorithmic playlist placement as a standalone revenue line.

Strategic logic: Even a credible threat shifts bargaining position at label licence renewal. The goal is leverage, not full disintermediation.

R4 · Regime ↑

Shape the Rules

The appropriability regime is not fixed. Changing the rules of the game is itself a strategy.

Apple Antitrust — Highest Priority

EU DMA enforcement would structurally remove the 30% iOS tax overnight.

Multi-Front Campaign

US (FTC, DOJ), EU, and UK — shifting the global norm on gatekeeper liability.

Streaming Coalition

United front with Deezer, Tidal, Soundcloud is more effective than individual litigation.

Royalty Rate Reviews

Per-stream rates set periodically by tribunals. Active participation is a legitimate strategic tool.

R5 · Assets ↑

Ecosystem Partnerships

Spotify cannot build iPhones — but it can create bilateral dependencies outside Apple's and Amazon's control.

Automotive · Highest priority

In-car audio is large and growing. Car OEMs not yet locked into Apple/Android ecosystems.

Tencent Model

2017 equity swap: bilateral ownership created mutual market access. Replicate in SE Asia, LATAM, South Asia.

Gaming

Deep PlayStation/Xbox integration. Gaming is a growing listening context with bilateral dependency potential.

Part Four
Lessons for

Key theoretical and practical implications
Lesson 1

Enabling Tech: The Value Trap

~74%
Revenue to rights holders per euro
€0
Annual profit despite €7.88B revenue
32%
Global share — yet value flows elsewhere

Teece (2018): Enabling technologies face systematically weaker value capture. Social returns typically double private returns. The broad applicability that makes enabling tech transformative prevents the innovator from owning all complements.

→ Platform innovators must design appropriability strategies from inception.

Lesson 2

Assets Trump Innovation

Spotify — The Innovator
  • Created the dominant design
  • 356M MAUs, 158M premium
  • Net loss every year since founding
  • Market leadership ≠ profitability
Apple Music — The Asset Holder
  • Replicated design within months
  • 10M subscribers in 6 weeks via iOS
  • Music is a loss-leader for hardware
  • Asset control > product innovation

Generalised lesson: Market entry decisions should prioritise complementary asset analysis over product superiority. A technically inferior product backed by superior assets will consistently outperform.

Lesson 3

Shaping the Rules

The appropriability regime is not fixed — it can be shaped through litigation, lobbying, and standard-setting.

Digital Platforms

Regulatory engagement IS strategy. The EU DMA could eliminate the 30% iOS tax — more impactful than any product update.

Emerging Markets

Treat the regulatory environment as a strategic variable to shape, not merely accept.

MNC Strategy

Cross-jurisdictional regulatory arbitrage is a capability. Spotify leveraged EU law against a US firm.

Lesson 4

Diversify to Capture

FROM: Music-Only

Weak appropriability. ~74% to rights holders. Fully imitable. No proprietary moat.

↓
TO: Audio Ecosystem

Owned podcast IP. No label royalties. Exclusive content. Creator lock-in. Audiobooks.

The Generalised Lesson

When core domain has structural appropriability limits, diversify into adjacent verticals where proprietary assets can be built.

Spotify's ~€1B podcast investment is a structural appropriability repair programme.
